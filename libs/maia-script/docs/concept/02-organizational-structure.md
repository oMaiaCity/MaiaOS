# Organizational Structure

## Entity Hierarchy

The Maia ecosystem operates through a hierarchical structure of legal entities, each serving specific purposes within the overall mission.

```
Maia Garden e.V. (Parent Organization)
└── Maia Holding GmbH (100% Subsidiary)
    └── Maia City GmbH (Operating Company)
        └── Product Divisions
```

## Entity Descriptions

### Maia Garden e.V.

**Type**: Eingetragener Verein (Registered Non-Profit Association) - German legal structure

**Role**: Brand, IP Asset Holder, and Maia AI Creator Entity

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Stewardship of the Maia brand identity
- Intellectual property management and protection
- Maia AI research, development, and ethical oversight
- Governance and guardianship of all subsidiary divisions
- Democratic decision-making structure
- Community representation and stakeholder engagement

**Governance Model**: Operates under democratization principles with member-based decision-making processes, ensuring the organization remains true to its mission and values.

**Legal Jurisdiction**: Germany (e.V. structure)

---

### Maia Holding GmbH

**Type**: Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung (Limited Liability Company)

**Ownership**: 100% subsidiary of Maia Garden e.V.

**Role**: Organizational Management and Asset Holding Entity

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Management of all future Maia City physical assets
- Real estate acquisition, development, and management
- Housing infrastructure oversight
- City infrastructure asset management
- Financial management and treasury operations
- Investment vehicle for Maia Founder tier participants

**Asset Categories**:
1. **Real Estate**: Land holdings, commercial properties, residential developments
2. **Infrastructure**: Transportation, utilities, public spaces
3. **Housing**: Residential buildings and community living spaces
4. **Technology Infrastructure**: Data centers, connectivity systems
5. **Investment Portfolio**: Strategic financial assets

**Legal Jurisdiction**: Germany (GmbH structure)

---

### Maia City GmbH

**Type**: Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung (Limited Liability Company)

**Former Name**: Visioncreator GmbH (to be renamed)

**Role**: Operating Company for AI-Native Infrastructure and Platforms

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Development of AI-native infrastructures and platforms
- Coordination of Maia City operations
- Market-facing brand management for Maia AI and Maia Garden
- Product development and delivery
- Social media management for all Maia Garden channels
- Customer relationship management
- Revenue generation through product offerings

**Product Portfolio**:
1. Maia Universe (Free tier)
2. Maia Voice (Subscription tier)
3. Maia Creator Academy (Educational tier)
4. Maia Founder (Investment tier)

**Go-to-Market Strategy**: Phased product launch starting with Maia Universe as the entry point, building toward the full ecosystem of offerings.

**Legal Jurisdiction**: Germany (GmbH structure)

## Organizational Design Rationale

### Why This Structure?

**Separation of Concerns**:
- **Maia Garden e.V.**: Non-profit ideological and IP stewardship
- **Maia Holding GmbH**: Asset protection and financial management
- **Maia City GmbH**: Operational execution and market engagement

**Legal Protection**:
- Multiple entity layers protect core mission and assets
- Limited liability structures reduce risk exposure
- German legal framework provides stability and credibility

**Governance Flexibility**:
- Democratic control at the top (e.V.)
- Professional management in the middle (Holding)
- Operational agility at the bottom (City)

**Investment Structure**:
- Clear pathway for Maia Founder investments
- Asset backing through Maia Holding GmbH
- Transparent value creation and distribution

### Future Evolution

As Maia City grows, additional entities may be established for:
- Regional city operations (Maia City Berlin GmbH, etc.)
- Specialized infrastructure projects
- International expansion vehicles
- Technology licensing subsidiaries

---

## Governance Principles

All entities operate under these shared governance principles:

1. **Transparency**: Open communication about operations, finances, and decision-making
2. **Accountability**: Clear responsibility assignment and performance measurement
3. **Democratic Input**: Community participation in strategic decisions (via Maia Garden e.V.)
4. **Professional Execution**: Competent management of operational entities
5. **Long-Term Thinking**: Decisions optimized for sustainability and multi-generational impact
